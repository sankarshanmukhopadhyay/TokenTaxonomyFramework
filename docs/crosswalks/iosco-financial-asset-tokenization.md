# IOSCO Financial Asset Tokenization Crosswalk

IOSCO's financial-asset tokenization work emphasizes market integrity, investor protection, settlement, custody, disclosure, and operational risk. TTF artifacts can support those concerns by making legal, operational, and evidence assumptions explicit.

| IOSCO risk theme | TTF v1.3.0 artifact response |
|---|---|
| Unclear nature of the investor claim | Describe the underlying legal instrument, legal claim, economic rights, and redemption or enforcement route. |
| Custody and asset safeguarding | Record custodian, custody model, segregation model, and verification evidence. |
| Settlement and finality uncertainty | Document settlement asset, finality point, rollback conditions, and lifecycle event evidence. |
| Technology and smart-contract risk | Link implementation references, audit reports, policy references, and control-message tests. |
| Interoperability gaps | Reference identifier schemes, transfer-control standards, and lifecycle event mappings. |
| Disclosure inconsistency | Attach disclosure, risk factor, and validation-report references. |

## Evidence Expectation

Financial-asset token artifacts should target at least the Silver assurance profile. Gold is appropriate where artifacts are used in production issuance, collateral, settlement, or market infrastructure workflows.
